I Remember... The Birth of Calvary Chapel

“It all began more than fifty years ago, when just a few of us gathered for the first time in a humble trailer court recreation room on Newport Boulevard in Costa Mesa, California. We stood on the threshold—the enormity of what was before us we could not have imagined in our wildest dreams. ‘The Lord was in this place, and we didn’t know it’–– this could have been said of the ten or twelve people who gathered that first Sunday on a spring morning in 1961.”

About the Author

In 1961, Sharon Fischer, along with her husband Hal, and their five-year-old daughter, Terri Ann, attended the first service of Calvary Chapel held in a mobile home park recreation room with ten others.

Sharon and Hal Fisher were among the few founding members who sought to fellowship regularly with other Christian Costa Mesa families in the early 1960s and formed Calvary Chapel of Costa Mesa. At the time, Harold Fisher was a young Costa Mesa police officer who saw his profession as a ministry as well. As his career grew in leadership influence (he later retired as chief of police), so did the tiny fellowship of Calvary Chapel begin to grow remarkably. Over the 54-year history of Calvary Chapel of Costa Mesa, many police officers served in their profession while contributing within the ministry.

In the early 1960s Chuck and Kay reached out to the destitute hippie generation around Costa Mesa and Newport Beach. This was not an attractive subculture that churches welcomed to services. Yet as local police officers saw this incredible need for these young people to hear about the Lord, so did Pastor Chuck and Kay Smith. Although not a demographic that would fill offering plates, these addicted searching youths were open to the cleansing power of the Word of God. Faithfully taught, the gospel message would bring thousands of these young people to believe in Christ as their Savior. Consequently, their liberty from addiction led many of them on to successful, productive lives, often in ministry.

As the Costa Mesa Police Department celebrates its sixty-year anniversary on December 16, 2013, there is a wonderful parallel of servant-based leadership demonstrated among the “Peace Makers” in both organizations. May the Lord continue to be honored in all that He has done so faithfully in Calvary Chapel and in Costa Mesa.

Tom Gazsi
Chief of Police
